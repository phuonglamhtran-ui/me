Assignment Overview

Question 3

Code Input:

Insights

Comprehensive analysis of salary patterns, experience, and business implications.

▶️ Show / Hide All Insights
1
Experience Drives Salary More Than Age: Salary has a strong correlation with experience 0.83 but only a moderate correlation with age 0.52. Skills and tenure are more valuable than age.
Implication: Focus on experience-based career paths and skill development.
2
Salary Growth Accelerates After Mid-Level Experience: Upper salary quartile begins around 6.6 years. Compensation increases faster after mid-level roles.
Implication: Retention strategies should target employees approaching mid-career.
3
High Earners Are Only Slightly Older: Average age of high-salary employees: 33.4 years vs overall average 31.2. Experience, not age, drives compensation.
Implication: Early career mentoring can accelerate growth.
4
Workforce Concentrated in Medium–High Salary Bands: ~80% of employees are in medium/high salary categories.
Implication: Payroll planning should focus on performance-based rewards.
5
Data Quality Decisions Matter: Mean and constant-value imputation keeps dataset usable, but replacing experience with fixed values can underestimate variance.
Implication: Use robust methods (KNN, regression) for predictive modeling.
6
Practical Business Insight: Invest in experience-building opportunities rather than seniority alone.
Supports: Training, upskilling, clear career ladders, performance-linked compensation.
