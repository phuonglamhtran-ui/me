Report: User Information Behaviour & Data Protection Dashboard

Root Cause Analysis (RCA) of User Data Vulnerability

Author: Pansy Phuong Lam

Source: Source PDF

Dashboard: Public Tableau Dashboard

The Core Problem Identified

The core problem identified in the "User Information Behaviour" dashboard is a high discrepancy between users' concern for data privacy and their ability to protect themselves effectively. A Root Cause Analysis identifies three primary systemic failures that lead to user vulnerability, rather than mere user negligence:

1. Root Cause: Systemic Constraint (The "No Choice" Mandate)

This is the most direct cause of user failure to protect data, as cited by 93 respondents in Q20.

Platforms leverage market dominance to create take-it-or-leave-it consent models.
Effective consent is absent. Users are coerced into sharing data, making self-protection impossible when data collection is a mandatory precondition for service access.
Root Cause
Platforms' business models require mandatory, broad data collection for personalization and advertising revenue.
The entire platform economy is built on a foundation of surveillance capitalism. where data harvesting is non-negotiable for service delivery.

2. Root Cause: Cognitive Burden (Complexity and Low Literacy)

The complexity of platform tools and policies creates an overwhelming burden, leading to user apathy and inaction.

Platform interfaces are deliberately opaque: Privacy settings are often buried, technical, and require excessive effort to manage. Policies are written in dense legal jargon.
Users are forced to default to inaction (Rarely or Never updating settings, Q11) because the effort required to manage privacy outweighs the perceived benefit.
Root Cause
Lack of mandated, standardized, and user-friendly communication of privacy policies and controls. Users lack foundational digital privacy literacy, a gap that educational institutions have not adequately filled (Q29).

3. Root Cause: Governance Failure (Weak Accountability)

The lack of strong, visible regulatory enforcement undermines user confidence and shifts the burden entirely onto the individual.

***5 Whys Analysis: Why Do Users Fail to Protect Their Data?

The core problem is traced back to fundamental systemic and economic drivers:

Core Problem: Users Fail to Protect Their Data Effectively

  1. Why? (Proximate Cause: Constraint) Because users feel they have no choice but to accept broad data collection policies to access necessary digital services (Q20).
  2. Why? (Systemic Requirement) Because the platform's business model requires mandatory, broad data collection. They harvest personal data as the fundamental ingredient for highly profitable targeted advertising (Surveillance Capitalism).
  3. Why? (Economic Incentive) Because the value of hyper-personalized advertising far outweighs the current legal and reputational costs of broad data collection. The economic incentive to gather more data is greater than the incentive to respect privacy.
  4. Why? (Regulatory/Governance Failure) Because the regulatory frameworks are insufficient to counteract the power and complexity of global technology companies (Q26, Q28). Regulations are often slow, difficult to enforce, and do not impose high enough penalties to act as a deterrent.
  5. Why? (Foundational Failure) Because governments and regulatory bodies prioritize rapid technological advancement and economic growth over the fundamental digital rights of individuals. This foundational imbalance allows the "take-it-or-leave-it" consent model to persist as the global default.
