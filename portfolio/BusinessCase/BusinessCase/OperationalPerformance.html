Operational Performance & Impact Analysis Report

Fleet & Warehouse Optimization Using Telematics Data

By: Pansy Phuong Lam

Executive Summary

This case study analyzes over 550,000 telematics records from forklift and truck fleets across two major clients. Insights reveal safety risks, operational inefficiencies, driver behavior patterns, and equipment misuse. The findings highlight opportunities for fleet optimization, reduced maintenance costs, improved battery and equipment health, enhanced worker safety, and increased operational efficiency.

View on Tableau Public

Background

A large industrial client manages 1,066 forklifts and 392 trucks. Telematics data includes shock events, utilization logs, driver behavior, battery usage, spatial coordinates, and shipment operations. Rising maintenance expenses and safety concerns prompted a full analysis to identify root causes and propose data-driven improvements.

Root Cause Analysis (5 Whys Summary)

Problem: Frequent shock hotspots in narrow aisles and blind spots.

Root Cause: Outdated facility layout not aligned with current traffic volumes.

  • Growth in operations increased traffic density but layout remained unchanged.
  • No periodic layout reassessment or safety audits.

Problem: High shocks during Julyâ€“August.

Root Cause: Fatigue from insufficient staffing during high-demand periods.

  • Overtime and peak-season demand leading to fatigue.
  • No dynamic workforce planning model.

Problem: Multi-day login sessions.

Root Cause: Manual login/logout system with no automation or enforcement.

  • Manual logout relies on driver discipline, operators forget to log out.
  • Telemetry policies have not been updated for automated authentication.

Problem: Some routes show extremely high shock levels.

Root Cause: No standardized routing strategy or real-time GPS route control.

  • Road conditions vary and logistics partners use inconsistent paths.
  • Fleet lacks unified GPS routing enforcement.

Problem: Inconsistent battery health across fleet.

Root Cause: No consistent or enforced battery charging policy.

  • Operators follow different charging habits; lack of training and monitoring dashboards.
  • Battery lifecycle management process is not established.

Problem: Certain machines suffer more wear and damage.

Root Cause: Manual fleet allocation causing uneven equipment distribution.

  • Shift leaders assign machines unevenly; planning relies on manual decision-making.
  • Fleet management lacks optimized utilization rules.

Problem: Anomalous shock spikes.

Root Cause: Lack of preventive calibration management for telemetry sensors.

  • Calibration is irregular; maintenance team reacts only when issues appear.
  • Preventive telemetry maintenance process is missing.
