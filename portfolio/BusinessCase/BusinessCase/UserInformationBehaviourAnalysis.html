Professional Report: User Information Behaviour & Data Protection

Author: Han Tran

Open Research PDF Open Dashboard

1. Introduction

Digital platforms increasingly rely on user data for personalization, monetization, and engagement. Concurrently, privacy regulations such as GDPR and CCPA, along with growing user awareness, demand robust data protection measures. This report presents insights from a dashboard visualizing user information behaviour, derived from a comprehensive questionnaire survey. It highlights how awareness, trust, cultural norms, and technological adoption shape privacy attitudes and practices.

2. Summary and Analysis

This document summarizes and analyzes user information behavior, focusing on demographics, usage frequency, security concerns, awareness, and attitudes toward regulation. The survey includes 129 respondents from 42 countries across 7 continents.

3. Demographics & Digital Usage

Age and Employment

  • Age Groups: Largest 18-24 (42.64%, 55 respondents), followed by 25-34 (31.78%, 41 respondents).
  • Employment Status: Majority Students (55.81%, 72 respondents), followed by Employed (33.33%, 43 respondents).

4. Security Concerns & Awareness

Concern About Data Security

  • 87.6% of respondents are concerned about data security:
    • • Very Concerned: 33.33% (43 respondents).
    • • Somewhat Concerned: 27.13% (35 respondents)
    • • Concerned: 27.13% (35 respondents)
    • • Not very concerned: 11.63% (15 respondents)

Awareness of Privacy Settings

  • 58.91% (76 respondents) know how to adjust privacy settings on some apps.
  • 48.84% (63 respondents) know they can adjust location, camera, and microphone permissions.

Control over Shared Information

  • Only 5.43% (7 respondents) believe they effectively protect personal information; 37.21% (48 respondents) are "Sure (Just 50%)".
  • Main barriers:
    • • Must accept or agree to get information (93 respondents)
    • • Privacy policies/settings are difficult to understand (75 respondents)

5. Experience & Regulatory Attitudes

Experience with Scams/Spam

  • 91.47% (118 respondents) experienced spam via calls, messages, emails, or pop-ups.
  • Scams: Frequently encountered Sometimes (25%) or Occasionally (25%).

Reading Privacy Policies

  • Never read: 37.98% (49 respondents)
  • Sometimes read: 34.10% (44 respondents)
  • Yes read: 8.53% (11 respondents)

Regulation and Responsibility

  • 87.6% agree individuals must control collection and use of personal data.
  • 88.37% agree there should be stricter data regulations.
  • Primary responsibility for data protection: Companies/Organizations (90), National Government (78), Individuals (77).
